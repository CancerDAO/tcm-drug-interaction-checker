# Grapefruit Juice-Drug Interactions

## Mechanism of Action

Grapefruit juice contains furanocoumarins (primarily bergamottin and 6',7'-dihydroxybergamottin) that **irreversibly inhibit CYP3A4** enzymes in the intestinal wall. This enzyme is responsible for the first-pass metabolism of many orally administered drugs.

**Key pharmacological consequences:**
- **Increased drug bioavailability**: With CYP3A4 inhibited, more parent drug enters systemic circulation unchanged
- **No effect on IV medications**: Grapefruit juice does not affect drugs administered intravenously since the intestinal CYP3A4 is bypassed
- **Variable severity**: The magnitude of interaction depends on the drug's CYP3A4 dependency and intestinal enzyme content

## Cancer Drugs Affected

### Hormonal Therapies
| Drug | Mechanism | Clinical Concern |
|------|-----------|------------------|
| **Tamoxifen** | CYP3A4 metabolism | Increased active metabolite exposure possible |
| **Toromifene** | CYP3A4 substrate | Potential increased serum levels |

### Targeted Therapies
| Drug | Mechanism | Clinical Concern |
|------|-----------|------------------|
| **Crizotinib** | CYP3A4 substrate | Increased exposure, potential toxicity |
| **Erlotinib** | CYP3A4 metabolism | Altered drug levels |
| **Sunitinib** | CYP3A4 substrate | Increased sunitinib exposure |
| **Dasatinib** | CYP3A4 substrate | Significantly increased bioavailability |
| **Nilotinib** | CYP3A4 substrate | Increased AUC |
| **Everolimus** | CYP3A4 substrate | Major interaction - increased levels |
| **Sirolimus (rapamycin)** | CYP3A4 substrate | Major interaction |

### Chemotherapy (less common)
Most IV chemotherapy is not significantly affected by grapefruit juice since it bypasses intestinal metabolism. However, oral chemotherapy agents may be affected.

## Severity Classification

| Severity | Definition | Action |
|----------|------------|--------|
| **Major** | Potentially life-threatening; avoid concurrent use | Contraindicated combination |
| **Moderate** | May require dose adjustment or monitoring | Use with caution, monitor |
| **Minor** | Minimal clinical effect; monitor for symptoms | Generally safe, be aware |

## Clinical Recommendations

1. **Avoid grapefruit juice** and grapefruit-containing products for drugs with known CYP3A4 interactions
2. **Check labeling** - FDA requires grapefruit interaction warnings on affected drug labels
3. **Consider alternatives** - Orange juice does not contain furanocoumarins and does not cause these interactions
4. **Timing matters** - The inhibitory effect persists for 24+ hours after grapefruit consumption
