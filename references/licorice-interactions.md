# Licorice (甘草) Drug Interactions

## Overview

Licorice root (Glycyrrhiza glabra) contains glycyrrhizin (glycyrrhizic acid), which is metabolized to glycyrrhetic acid. This compound inhibits 11-beta-hydroxysteroid dehydrogenase type 2, leading to mineralocorticoid effects.

## Key Interaction Mechanism

### Pseudoaldosteronism (假性醛固酮增多症)

**Glycyrrhizin** blocks the breakdown of cortisol to cortisone in the kidney, causing:
- Sodium retention
- Potassium loss
- Water retention
- Hypertension
- Edema
- Arrhythmias

### Risk Factors for Severity
- Daily intake > 50g licorice for > 2 weeks
- Individual susceptibility varies
- Low potassium diet
- Pre-existing hypertension or heart disease

## Drug Interactions

### Cardiovascular Drugs

| Drug | Interaction | Mechanism | Severity |
|------|-------------|-----------|----------|
| **Digoxin** | Increased toxicity risk | Hypokalemia enhances digoxin effect | Major |
| **Antiarrhythmics** | Altered electrolyte effects | Potassium loss affects drug efficacy | Moderate-Major |
| **Antihypertensives** | Reduced efficacy | Sodium/water retention raises BP | Moderate |

### Diuretics
| Drug | Interaction | Concern |
|------|-------------|---------|
| **Thiazide diuretics** | Additive potassium loss | Severe hypokalemia risk |
| **Loop diuretics** | Additive potassium loss | Severe hypokalemia risk |
| **Potassium-sparing** | Partially protective | Monitor potassium |

### Corticosteroids
- Additive mineralocorticoid effects
- Increased hypertension and edema risk
- Monitor closely if concurrent use necessary

### Warfarin/Anticoagulants
- Generally no direct interaction
- Indirect effect through electrolyte changes affecting INR
- Monitor INR if licorice use is initiated/discontinued

## Cancer-Specific Considerations

### Steroid-Responsive Cancers
Licorice may have glucocorticoid-like effects:
- Theoretical concern in steroid-responsive leukemias
- May complicate management of lymphomas treated with steroids

### Hypertension Concerns
Many cancer patients have:
- Preexisting hypertension from therapy or disease
- Steroid-induced hypertension from antiemetics
- licorice use may worsen these conditions

## Cancer Cachexia and Appetite

Some traditional uses of licorice in Chinese medicine:
- Appetite stimulation
- Fatigue reduction
- These uses are **not evidence-based** for cancer cachexia

## Deglycyrrhizinated Licorice (DGL)

DGL has removed the glycyrrhizin component:
- **No pseudoaldosteronism effect**
- May have some GI protective effects
- Does not interact with cardiovascular medications
- Safer alternative when licorice is desired for GI symptoms

## Clinical Recommendations

### For Patients with Cardiovascular Disease
1. **Avoid licorice root** if history of hypertension, heart failure, arrhythmias
2. **Discuss with cardiologist** before use in any cardiac patient
3. **Monitor blood pressure** if use is unavoidable

### For Cancer Patients
1. **Avoid licorice** during treatment due to electrolyte effects
2. **Consider DGL** if GI benefits needed
3. **Tell oncologist** about any licorice-containing products

### Monitoring Parameters
If licorice use is continued despite warnings:
- Blood pressure (baseline, then weekly)
- Serum potassium (baseline, then periodic)
- Electrolytes
- Signs of fluid retention
