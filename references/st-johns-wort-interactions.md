# St. John's Wort (Hypericum perforatum) Interactions

## Mechanism of Action

St. John's Wort is a potent **CYP450 enzyme inducer**, primarily affecting:

### Enzyme Induction Targets
- **CYP3A4** - Most clinically significant; induces intestinal and hepatic CYP3A4
- **CYP2C9** - Warfarin and NSAID metabolism
- **CYP2C19** - Proton pump inhibitors, some antidepressants
- **P-glycoprotein (P-gp)** - Drug efflux transporter in gut and blood-brain barrier

**Pharmacological consequence:**
Enzyme induction leads to **decreased drug plasma concentrations**, reduced therapeutic effect, and potential treatment failure. The induction typically requires 1-3 weeks of continuous St. John's Wort use to reach maximal effect.

## Cancer Drugs Affected

| Drug Class | Specific Drugs | Effect |
|------------|-----------------|--------|
| **Hormonal therapies** | Tamoxifen | Reduced efficacy - CYP3A4 induction increases tamoxifen metabolism to less active metabolites |
| **Chemotherapy** | Irinotecan | Reduced active metabolite (SN-38) levels, decreased efficacy |
| **Targeted therapies** | Imatinib | Increased metabolism, reduced trough levels |
| **Supportive care** | Oxycodone | Reduced analgesia due to CYP3A4 induction |

## Major Drug Interaction Categories

### Antidepressants (SSRIs/SNRIs)
- **Contraindicated** with serotonergic drugs due to serotonin syndrome risk
- Avoid combination with: fluoxetine, sertraline, paroxetine, venlafaxine

### Anticoagulants
| Drug | Interaction |
|------|-------------|
| **Warfarin** | Significantly reduced warfarin levels - INR monitoring essential |
| **Apixaban** | Reduced levels by ~50% - avoid combination |
| **Rivaroxaban** | Reduced levels - avoid combination |
| **Dabigatran** | Reduced efficacy possible |

### Cardiovascular Drugs
- **Digoxin** - P-gp induction reduces digoxin levels
- **Simvastatin** - Reduced statin levels, may lose lipid-lowering effect
- **Amlodipine** - Reduced antihypertensive effect

### Immunosuppressants
- **Cyclosporine** - Dramatically reduced levels - transplant rejection risk
- **Tacrolimus** - Reduced levels - organ rejection risk
- **Mycophenolate** - Reduced levels

### Other Significant Interactions
- Oral contraceptives (reduced efficacy - breakthrough bleeding, pregnancy risk)
- Methadone (reduced levels - withdrawal symptoms)
- Anticonvulsants (complex interactions)

## Severity Classification

| Severity | Mechanism | Clinical Implication |
|----------|------------|---------------------|
| **Major** | Potentially life-threatening treatment failure or toxicity | **Avoid concurrent use** |
| **Moderate** | Significant effect requiring monitoring/dose adjustment | Monitor closely, may need dose adjustment |
| **Minor** | Mild effect; clinical observation | Be aware, monitor for reduced effect |

## Clinical Recommendations

1. **Discontinue St. John's Wort 2 weeks before** starting interacting medications
2. **Never combine with SSRIs/SNRIs** - Serotonin syndrome risk
3. **Monitor drug levels** for drugs with narrow therapeutic indices when starting/stopping St. John's Wort
4. **For cancer patients** - Avoid use during active cancer treatment due to unpredictable effects on drug metabolism
