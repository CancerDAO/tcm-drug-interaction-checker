# Cordyceps (虫草) and Ganoderma/Lingzhi (灵芝) Drug Interactions

## Cordyceps (冬虫夏草)

### Overview
Cordyceps sinensis (now classified as Ophiocordyceps sinensis) is a parasitic fungus used in traditional Chinese medicine for its purported benefits in fatigue, respiratory conditions, and immune modulation.

### Mechanism of Action
- **Immunomodulatory effects**: Can stimulate both Th1 and Th2 immune responses
- **Potential immunosuppressive effects at high doses**: Paradoxical immunomodulation
- **No significant CYP450 interactions** reported in literature

### Drug Interactions

| Drug Category | Interaction | Clinical Concern |
|---------------|-------------|------------------|
| **Immunosuppressants** (cyclosporine, tacrolimus) | Additive immunosuppression | Moderate - monitor for over-immunosuppression |
| **Corticosteroids** | Additive effects | Theoretical concern |
| **Anticoagulants** | Minimal direct effect | Low concern |

### Cancer-Specific Considerations
- May theoretically interfere with immunosuppression needed during chemotherapy
- Generally considered safe but discuss with oncologist
- Limited clinical evidence for claimed benefits

## Ganoderma/Lingzhi (灵芝)

### Overview
Ganoderma lucidum (reishi mushroom) is used in TCM for immune enhancement, fatigue reduction, and general wellness. Widely studied for potential anticancer applications.

### Mechanism of Action

#### Immunological Effects
- **Beta-glucan content**: Potent immunostimulant
- **T-cell activation**: May enhance cellular immunity
- **Cytokine modulation**: Both pro- and anti-inflammatory effects
- **Potential anti-angiogenic effects**: Preliminary research

#### Metabolic Effects
- **CYP450 interactions**: Limited data but theoretical concern
- **P-glycoprotein**: May affect drug efflux pumps

### Drug Interactions

| Drug | Interaction | Clinical Concern |
|------|-------------|------------------|
| **Anticoagulants** (warfarin, heparin) | Potential additive antiplatelet effects | Moderate - monitor bleeding |
| **Antihypertensives** | May have additive blood pressure lowering | Minor - monitor BP |
| **Immunosuppressants** | May antagonize immunosuppressive therapy | Moderate - discuss with transplant team |
| **Chemotherapy** | Theoretical beneficial or interfering effects | Variable - discuss with oncologist |

### Bleeding Risk
Ganoderma has documented antiplatelet activity:
- May prolong bleeding time
- Case reports of increased INR when combined with warfarin
- Discontinue 2 weeks before surgery

### Cancer-Specific Considerations

#### Potential Benefits
- Quality of life improvement in some studies
- Potential synergistic effects with chemotherapy (preclinical)
- May reduce chemotherapy-induced fatigue

#### Potential Concerns
- May stimulate immune response in hematologic malignancies
- Limited evidence for claimed anticancer effects
- Variable product quality

## Combined Use Considerations

When patients use both Cordyceps and Ganoderma:

1. **Additive immunomodulatory effects** - may amplify immune response
2. **Potential for immune overstimulation** in autoimmune conditions or certain cancers
3. **Limited clinical data** on combined use

## Clinical Recommendations

### General
1. Discuss all supplement use with treating physician
2. Quality control varies widely among products
3. Consider potential interactions with specific treatment regimens

### For Cancer Patients
1. **During active treatment**: Discuss with oncologist before use
2. **Post-treatment**: Generally lower concern but discuss with care team
3. **Surgery planning**: Discontinue 2 weeks before elective surgery
